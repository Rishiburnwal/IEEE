Taj Mahal

An immense mausoleum of white marble, built in Agra between 1631 and 1648 by order
of the Mughal emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his favourite wife, the Taj Mahal is
the jewel of Muslim art in India and one of the universally admired masterpieces
of the world's heritage.The white marble structure, adorned with intricate carvings
and inlaid with precious stonesis widely considered one of the most beautiful buildings
in the world and is a UNESCO World Heritage Site. It is also one of the most popular tourist
attractions in India, drawing millions of visitors each year.

The Taj Mahal is located in the Indian city of Agra, in the northern state of Uttar Pradesh.
It is situated on the southern bank of the Yamuna River, about 210 kilometers (130 miles)
southeast of the national capital, New Delhi. The exact address of the Taj Mahal is: Taj Mahal,
Dharmapuri, Forest Colony, Tajganj, Agra, Uttar Pradesh 282001, India.

---By Nana Patnekar

Red Fort

The Red Fort, also known as the "Lal Qila," is a historic fort located in the Indian city of Delhi.
of Delhi.It was built by the Mughal emperor Shah Jahan in the mid-17th century and served as the
main residence of the Mughal emperors for nearly 200 years. The fort is made of red sandstone
and is an excellent example of Mughal architecture, with intricate carvings and designs adorning
its walls.The fort has several important structures within its walls, including the Diwan-i-Am,
the Diwan-i-Khas, the Rang Mahal, the Khas Mahal, and the Moti Masjid. It is also a UNESCO World
Heritage Site. The Red Fort is considered one of the most important symbols of India's history and
heritage, and it is a popular tourist destination in Delhi.

The Red Fort is located in the Indian city of Delhi, the capital of India. The fort is situated
in the Old City of Delhi, on the eastern bank of the Yamuna River. The address of the Red Fort
s: Netaji Subhash Marg, Lahori Gate, Chandni Chowk, New Delhi, Delhi 110006, India. It's easily
accessible by public transportation and is a short walk from the Chandni Chowk metro station.
The Red Fort is also one of the most popular tourist attractions in Delhi, and it is usually
crowded with visitors, especially during the Independence Day (15th of August) celebrations and
other national holidays.

Hawa Mahal

The Hawa Mahal, also known as the "Palace of Breeze," is a palace located in Jaipur, Rajasthan,
India. It was built in 1799 by Maharaja Sawai Pratap Singh, who ruled the Kingdom of Jaipur from
1778 to 1803. The palace was designed by the court architect, Lal Chand Ustad, in the form of the
crown of Lord Krishna, the Hindu god.

The palace is situated on the edge of the City Palace, Jaipur and extends to the zenana, or women's
chambers. It is made of pink sandstone and is a five-story structure with a pyramidal shape and 953
small windows or "jharokhas" that are decorated with intricate latticework. These jharokhas allowed
the women of the royal household to watch daily life and processions in the streets below without being seen.

Hawa Mahal was built as a summer palace for the royal ladies, and it also served as a place for them to take
part in the processions and ceremonies of the court. Today, it is a major tourist attraction in Jaipur and a
popular spot for photography. The palace is open to visitors, and there is a small museum inside that displays
various artifacts related to the history of the palace and the Kingdom of Jaipur.

----By Raja Jai Singhl
